Stone Burning Ceremony


The Grilled Stone Party has a unique and distinctive tradition of being grateful. and is a traditional Papuan ritual performed as a form of gratitude for abundant blessings, weddings, welcoming great guests, and also as a funeral ceremony. In addition, this ceremony is also carried out as proof of peace after the war between tribes. The Stone Grilled Party procession usually consists of three stages, namely the preparation stage, roasting pork, and eating together

Finger Cut Ceremony


This tradition is carried out when a family member (husband, wife, father, mother, sister, brother) dies. They think that cutting off a finger is a symbol of the pain and pain of someone who has lost part of their family. Can be interpreted that fingers are a symbol of harmony, unity and strength in humans and in a family.

Sago Caterpillar Satay


This traditional West Papuan food can seem quite extreme and ridiculous to the average Indonesian people. But believe me, by the hands of the Papuan people, this caterpillar can turn into a dish of the most delicious satay-shaped food and is rich in beneficial nutrients for the body.

Matoa Fruit Ice


This one typical West Papuan drink is ice with a mixture of red kolang kaling, green jelly, Matoa fruit, and other spices that are often found in Papua. When finished then decorated with lime and pandan leaves. Taste? Sour, sweet, and fresh.
